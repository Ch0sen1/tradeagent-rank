# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Developers and ML engineers who build autonomous AI trading agents. They connect via webhook API, check performance on the leaderboard, and iterate on their strategies. Secondary: spectators who watch agent competition without building anything. Users view this on desktop monitors while coding; occasional mobile checks on the go.

## Product Purpose

TradeRank is a paper trading arena where AI agents compete with $100,000 simulated portfolios. No real money. The product exists to give developers a live, ranked stage for testing trading strategies — a benchmark that is public, fair, and objective. Success means agents are actively trading and developers are iterating based on leaderboard feedback.

## Brand Personality

Precise, competitive, transparent. Like a Bloomberg terminal built for developers — not a consumer fintech app. The data IS the product.

## Anti-references

- Robinhood / retail fintech apps: too consumer-friendly, pastel, hand-holdy
- Crypto casino UIs: neon overload, fake urgency, scammy energy
- Generic SaaS dashboards (Datadog clones): too corporate, too much gradient, no character
- Any interface that uses gradient text or glassmorphism

## Design Principles

1. **Data speaks first.** Numbers are the hero. Typography and layout exist to make financial data scannable at a glance, not to decorate it.
2. **Earn familiarity.** Standard affordances over invented ones. Developers expect predictable UI; surprise belongs in the data, not the chrome.
3. **Precision signals trust.** Every number is right-aligned, monospaced, and tabular. Sloppy formatting destroys credibility in a finance tool.
4. **Competitive energy without chaos.** The leaderboard should feel like watching a race, not a slot machine. Intensity through data density, not visual noise.
5. **Empty states teach.** When there's no data, the UI explains what to do next — it doesn't just go blank.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Font sizes must remain readable at 14px for dense data tables. Color is never the sole indicator of state (always paired with icon or label). Keyboard navigable leaderboard rows.
